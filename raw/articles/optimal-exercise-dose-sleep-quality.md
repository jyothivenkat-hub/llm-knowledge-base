# Optimal Exercise Dose and Type for Improving Sleep Quality

## Abstract

This systematic review and network meta-analysis investigated which specific exercise programs most effectively improve sleep quality by examining exercise types and dosages (duration, intervention period, frequency, intensity). The study analyzed 58 randomized controlled trials encompassing 5,008 participants.

## Key Findings

### Exercise Frequency Effects on Sleep
- Exercising 4 times per week is the most effective frequency for improving sleep quality (SUCRA = 84.7)
- 1-2 times weekly: SMD = -0.85, 95% CI (-1.43, -0.26)
- 3 times weekly: SMD = -0.45, 95% CI (-0.80, -0.11)
- 4 times weekly: SMD = -1.09, 95% CI (-1.92, -0.26)

### Session Duration
- Sessions of 30 minutes or less significantly outperformed 40-55 minute sessions (SMD = 0.75)
- Short, focused exercise bouts appear more beneficial for sleep than longer sessions
- The sweet spot is 30 minutes or less per session (SUCRA = 92.2)

### Optimal Intervention Period
- 9-10 weeks of consistent exercise produced the strongest sleep improvements (SUCRA = 89.9)
- 9-10 weeks: SMD = -1.40, 95% CI (-2.37, -0.44) — most effective period
- 12-16 weeks: SMD = -0.55, 95% CI (-0.90, -0.20)
- 24+ weeks: SMD = -0.71, 95% CI (-1.31, -0.10)
- The 9-10 week window significantly outperformed 6-8 weeks

### Exercise Intensity
- High intensity exercise was most effective for sleep (SUCRA = 92.9)
- Moderate intensity: SMD = -1.06
- High intensity: SMD = -1.48
- Both high and moderate intensity were superior to moderate-high intensity

### Exercise Types
- Combined exercise (aerobic + resistance) was most effective (SUCRA = 82.7)
- Aerobic exercise: SMD = -0.56
- Traditional Chinese exercises (tai chi, qigong): SMD = -0.57
- Combined exercise: SMD = -0.99

## The Optimal Exercise Prescription for Sleep

The most effective combination for improving sleep quality:
- Frequency: 4 times per week
- Duration: 30 minutes or less per session
- Period: 9-10 weeks of consistent practice
- Intensity: High intensity
- Type: Combined exercise (aerobic + resistance training)

## Mechanisms

Elevated levels of endorphins from exercise mitigate stress and anxiety, induce relaxation, and regulate circadian rhythms, ultimately enhancing sleep quality. Exercise also increases adenosine accumulation (the molecule that builds sleep pressure) and raises core body temperature, with the subsequent cooling promoting sleep onset.

## Methodology

- Databases searched: Web of Science, PubMed, Cochrane Library, Embase, Scopus (cutoff April 30, 2024)
- Analysis tools: RevMan 5.4 for bias assessment, Stata 17.0 for network meta-analysis
- Quality ranking: Surface under cumulative ranking curve (SUCRA)
- 58 RCTs with 5,008 total participants

## Limitations

Limited number of studies for some exercise frequencies and types necessitates further research. Most studies used the Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index (PSQI) as the primary outcome measure, which is self-reported.
