# Middle layers of LLMs show stronger correlation with brain activity than final layers, particularly in language-selective regions like the inferior frontal gyrus and posterior temporal cortex, suggesting final layers over-optimize for task performance at the expense of brain-like representations.

**Type:** finding
**Source:** Do Large Language Models Think Like the Brain? Layer-Wise Embeddings and fMRI
**Evidence:** Key Findings section: 'Middle layers of LLMs show higher brain-model correlation than final layers' and Implications: 'final output layers of LLMs may over-optimize for task performance at the expense of brain-like representations'
