# 400mg caffeine consumed 4 hours before bedtime reduces total sleep time by 50.6 minutes and increases time to persistent sleep by 25.4 minutes, while 100mg produces no significant sleep disruption at any timing.

**Type:** finding
**Source:** Dose and Timing Effects of Caffeine on Subsequent Sleep: A Randomized Clinical Crossover Trial
**Evidence:** Key Findings section: 400mg reduces total sleep by 50.6 min at 4-hour timing; 100mg produces NO significant effect at any timing
