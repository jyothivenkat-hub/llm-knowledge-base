# Caffeine suppresses sleep through dual mechanisms: antagonizing adenosine receptors (A1 and A2A) to block sleep pressure accumulation, and suppressing melatonin secretion to delay the biological night.

**Type:** concept
**Source:** Dose and Timing Effects of Caffeine on Subsequent Sleep: A Randomized Clinical Crossover Trial
**Evidence:** Mechanism section: 'Caffeine acts by antagonizing adenosine receptors (A1 and A2A)... By blocking adenosine, caffeine artificially suppresses sleepiness. It also suppresses melatonin secretion and delays the biological night.'
