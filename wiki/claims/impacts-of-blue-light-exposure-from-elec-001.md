# Blue light wavelengths between 460-480 nm produce the most substantial melatonin suppression, with 2 hours of LED tablet exposure causing a 55% decrease in melatonin levels and a 1.5-hour delay in melatonin onset compared to reading printed material under low light.

**Type:** finding
**Source:** Impacts of Blue Light Exposure From Electronic Devices on Circadian Rhythm and Sleep Disruption in Adolescent and Young Adult Students
**Evidence:** Key Findings section: '55% decrease in melatonin levels' and '1.5 hours compared to reading a printed book' after 2-hour tablet exposure
