# Blue light disrupts sleep through a specific neural pathway: ipRGCs (intrinsically photosensitive retinal ganglion cells) maximally sensitive to 460-480 nm wavelengths signal the suprachiasmatic nucleus, which suppresses melatonin production in the pineal gland.

**Source:** Impacts of Blue Light Exposure From Electronic Devices on Circadian Rhythm and Sleep Disruption in Adolescent and Young Adult Students
