# Blue light blocking glasses show some benefit but do not fully compensate for evening screen exposure, making screen time reduction 2+ hours before bed the most effective intervention for preserving sleep quality.

**Type:** finding
**Source:** Impacts of Blue Light Exposure From Electronic Devices on Circadian Rhythm and Sleep Disruption in Adolescent and Young Adult Students
**Evidence:** Practical Implications section: 'Blue light blocking glasses show some benefit but don't fully compensate' and 'most effective intervention is simply reducing screen exposure 2+ hours before bed'
