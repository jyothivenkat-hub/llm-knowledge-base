# The relationship between exercise duration and sleep benefit is non-monotonic: the shortest effective duration (≤30 min) outperforms moderate durations (40-55 min), suggesting diminishing or negative returns beyond an optimal threshold rather than a linear dose-response.

**Type:** concept
**Source:** Optimal Exercise Dose and Type for Improving Sleep Quality: A Systematic Review and Network Meta-Analysis of RCTs
**Evidence:** Session Duration findings show 30 min sessions superior to 40-55 min (SMD = 0.75 difference), contradicting simple linear dose assumptions
