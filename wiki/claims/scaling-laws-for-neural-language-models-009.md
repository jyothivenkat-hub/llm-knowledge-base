# The revision from Kaplan et al. to Chinchilla scaling laws led to practical models (Chinchilla, LLaMA) that are smaller but better-trained, shifting the paradigm from maximizing model size to optimizing the compute-token-size tradeoff.

**Type:** finding
**Source:** Scaling Laws for Neural Language Models
**Evidence:** Chinchilla and LLaMA models adopted the revised scaling approach with smaller size and more training tokens
