## Overview

Modulation refers to the dynamic regulation of neural or computational signals by contextual factors operating at different timescales. In the context of language processing, modulation encompasses both the task-dependent alignment between artificial neural networks and biological brains, and the temporal hierarchies through which slower contextual signals regulate faster information processing events.

## Key Findings

### Language Proficiency as a Modulating Factor

[[Language proficiency]] acts as a critical modulator of how well large language models align with human brain activity. Research demonstrates that GPT-2 models show substantially stronger predictive alignment with language-related brain regions in proficient L2 (second language) users compared to independent or basic L2 users. This suggests that the neural signatures of language processing become more structured and consistent at higher proficiency levels, creating a stronger correspondence with the hierarchical organization of transformer architectures.

### Temporal Hierarchies in Attention Computation

A fundamental modulation mechanism emerges from the interaction between fast and slow neural timescales. [[Astrocytic calcium signaling]], which operates on slower timescales than [[neuronal firing]], provides a biological substrate for contextual modulation analogous to the learned [[attention weights]] in [[transformer blocks]]. This creates a natural temporal hierarchy where:

- **Fast layer**: Rapid synaptic events and neuronal firing encode immediate linguistic features
- **Slow layer**: Astrocytic calcium signaling provides contextual constraints that modulate which fast signals are amplified or suppressed

This two-timescale architecture mirrors how transformer attention mechanisms learn to weight different tokens based on broader context, suggesting that biological brains may implement attention-like computation through temporal modulation rather than explicit weight matrices.

## Methods

- **Comparative neuroscience**: Measuring alignment between LLM layer activations and fMRI/neural recordings across language proficiency levels
- **Biophysical modeling**: Characterizing the temporal dynamics of astrocytic calcium signaling relative to synaptic transmission
- **Computational analogy**: Mapping biological timescale hierarchies to architectural features of transformer models

## Open Questions

- How does modulation by language proficiency vary across different language pairs or linguistic structures?
- What specific mechanisms translate the temporal hierarchy of astrocytic-neuronal interaction into the learned attention patterns observed in transformers?
- Does modulation operate similarly in other cognitive domains beyond language, or is it language-specific?
- Can we identify explicit computational signatures of astrocytic modulation in neural recordings?
