## Overview

Wearables are portable biosensing devices that continuously monitor physiological metrics in real-world settings. In sleep and [[cognitive performance]] research, wearables like the Oura ring have emerged as powerful tools for capturing fine-grained sleep data previously inaccessible through traditional laboratory methods. These devices enable longitudinal tracking of sleep characteristics and their relationship to cognitive outcomes.

## Key Findings

### Sleep Metrics and Cognitive Performance

Wearables have revealed that [[cognitive performance]] is influenced by a multidimensional sleep profile rather than any single sleep metric. Specific sleep dimensions tracked by modern wearables—including night-time heart rate, sleep latency, sleep timing, sleep restfulness, and overall sleep quantity—all significantly influence cognitive outcomes. This finding suggests that comprehensive sleep assessment requires simultaneous analysis of multiple physiological dimensions rather than reliance on traditional measures like total sleep duration alone.

### Technological Capabilities

Modern sleep-tracking wearables provide unprecedented access to fine-grained sleep data. Metrics such as sleep stages, heart rate variability, and restfulness represent previously unmeasurable dimensions in sleep research, enabling discovery of novel relationships between sleep physiology and cognitive function that were not detectable in earlier laboratory-based research paradigms.

## Methods

Wearables have been integrated into [[longitudinal field studies]] combining multiple data modalities. A 2-month longitudinal design paired wearable sleep tracking (specifically the Oura ring) with complementary measures including smartphone typing metrics and active psychomotor vigilance testing (PVT). This multi-modal approach validates causal and correlational relationships between sleep characteristics and cognitive performance in naturalistic settings rather than controlled laboratory environments.

## Open Questions

- How do individual differences in physiology affect the reliability of wearable-derived sleep metrics?
- Which specific combinations of sleep dimensions are most predictive of particular cognitive domains?
- How do wearable metrics compare to gold-standard polysomnography in predicting real-world cognitive outcomes?
- Can wearable-based sleep interventions effectively improve cognitive performance?
