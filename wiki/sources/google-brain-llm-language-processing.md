## Brief
Google Research studied how human brains and large language models process natural language using intracranial electrode recordings during conversations. They found that neural activity aligns linearly with internal contextual embeddings from LLMs, revealing shared computational principles despite architectural differences.

## Summary

This 2025 Google Research study investigated the similarities between human brain language processing and large language model representations using [[intracranial-electrode-recordings]] during natural conversations. The research team extracted speech embeddings from the [[whisper-encoder]] and language embeddings from the [[whisper-decoder]] to analyze [[neural-language-alignment]].

The key finding demonstrates that neural activity in the human brain aligns linearly with the internal [[contextual-embeddings]] of speech and language within LLMs as they process everyday conversations. The study revealed a distinct [[speech-processing-sequence]]: during listening, speech embeddings predict activity in the [[superior-temporal-gyrus]] first, followed by language embeddings activating [[brocas-area]]. During speaking, this sequence reverses with language areas activating approximately 500ms before articulation, followed by motor cortex engagement.

The research identified shared computational principles between brains and LLMs, including [[next-word-prediction]] mechanisms, [[prediction-error-signals]] that generate surprise responses, and embedding-based contextual representation. The study also revealed a [[soft-hierarchy]] in brain processing where higher-order regions prioritize semantic information while still capturing acoustic features, and lower-order areas primarily process sound but also decode meaning, contrasting with strict feedforward models.

Despite finding striking representational alignment, the researchers noted key architectural differences: the brain processes language serially and temporally, while transformers handle thousands of words simultaneously.

## Key Concepts
- [[neural-language-alignment]] — Linear correspondence between human brain neural activity and LLM internal representations during language processing
- [[speech-processing-sequence]] — Temporal order of brain region activation during listening and speaking tasks
- [[next-word-prediction]] — Computational mechanism shared between brains and LLMs for language processing
- [[prediction-error-signals]] — Surprise responses generated when expectations don't match input
- [[contextual-embeddings]] — Vector representations that capture meaning based on surrounding context
- [[soft-hierarchy]] — Brain organization where regions have primary functions but also process multiple types of information
- [[superior-temporal-gyrus]] — Brain region that shows early activation during speech processing
- [[brocas-area]] — Language processing region that activates after superior temporal gyrus during listening
- [[intracranial-electrode-recordings]] — Direct neural measurement technique used to capture brain activity
- [[whisper-encoder]] — Component of OpenAI's Whisper model that processes speech input
- [[whisper-decoder]] — Component of OpenAI's Whisper model that generates language output
